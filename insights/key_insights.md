# 💡 Key Insights — Customer Churn Analysis

## 📊 Overview
- **Dataset:** 1,500 telecom customers
- **Overall Churn Rate:** ~31.8%
- **Analysis Focus:** Retention drivers and churn patterns

---

## 🔍 Key Findings

### 1. Contract Type is the Strongest Churn Predictor
- Month-to-Month customers have the highest churn (~45%)
- Two Year contract customers are the most loyal (~8% churn)
- Switching customers to longer contracts is the most impactful retention strategy

### 2. New Customers Are the Most Vulnerable
- Churn is highest in the first 6 months (~50%)
- Customers who stay beyond 36 months rarely leave (~12%)
- The first 3 months are the most critical retention window

### 3. Fiber Optic Service Has a Quality Problem
- Fiber Optic churn (~40%) is higher than DSL (~25%)
- High-paying customers are leaving — a significant revenue risk
- Service quality improvement is urgently needed

### 4. Payment Method Reflects Customer Commitment
- Electronic Check users churn ~40% — highest among payment types
- Auto-pay users (Bank Transfer, Credit Card) churn ~22%
- Encouraging auto-pay is a simple but effective retention lever

### 5. Product Bundling Reduces Churn
- Customers with 1 product churn significantly more
- Multi-product customers (3+) are far more loyal
- Bundle offers can increase stickiness

---

## 🚀 Business Recommendations

| Priority | Action | Expected Impact |
|----------|--------|----------------|
| 🔴 High | Discount incentives for annual contract conversion | -15% churn |
| 🔴 High | Structured 90-day onboarding for new customers | -20% early churn |
| 🟡 Medium | Fiber Optic quality audit & improvement | -10% fiber churn |
| 🟡 Medium | Cashback for switching to auto-pay | -8% payment churn |
| 🟢 Low | Product bundle offers for single-product users | +retention |
| 🟢 Low | Milestone loyalty rewards at 24 months | +long-term retention |
